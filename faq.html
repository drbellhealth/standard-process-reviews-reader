Standard Process FAQ

Quick answers to the questions visitors most often ask about Standard Process whole-food supplements (brand-wide review hub).

What do I need to know about Standard Process?

Standard Process is a Wisconsin supplement company that's been around since 1929. They grow a lot of their own ingredients on a 420-acre organic farm. The catalog is large — about 300 products. The big thing to know is that you can't just buy it; you have to go through a practitioner. That's the part that confuses most people who land here.

How do I actually buy it?

Through a chiropractor, naturopath, dietitian, integrative MD or DO, or acupuncturist who has a Standard Process account. They order it for you and usually mark it up a little. You should not buy it on Amazon — counterfeits and expired stock are real problems there, and the company actively goes after resellers.

Why is the whole-food angle a big deal?

Because most supplements you see at the store are synthetic isolates — pure vitamin C as ascorbic acid, B12 as cyanocobalamin, vitamin E as dl-alpha-tocopherol. Standard Process concentrates real food instead. So their vitamin C comes from acerola or alfalfa, their B vitamins come from nutritional yeast, and so on. The argument is that the original cofactors come along for the ride and help your body actually use the nutrient. The counter-argument is you get less per pill. Depends on what you're trying to do.

Are the glandulars going to be a problem for me?

Probably not, unless you have specific issues. The glandular products are made from bovine (beef) organ tissue at low doses. PMG products are made from organ nuclear material. For healthy adults, both are well-tolerated. If you have autoimmune thyroid disease, a beef allergy, or any active autoimmune condition, talk to your doctor before starting them. The beef comes from New Zealand BSE-free certified herds.

What might happen if I don't tolerate it?

The most common pattern is people doing the 21-Day Purification Program getting headaches, fatigue, and loose stools in the first few days. That's mostly the calorie cut, not the product per se. People on the glandulars sometimes feel wired or have trouble sleeping. People on the MediHerb herbal stuff occasionally get a reaction if they have ragweed allergies. None of these are dangerous; they're just signs to back off or adjust under your practitioner's guidance.

Is it actually worth what I'm paying?

Depends on the product. Catalyn — their flagship multi from 1929 — is unique and the price is fair. The MediHerb herbal line is genuinely competitive with the best practitioner-channel options. The glandulars and PMG line is unique to the brand. The 21-Day Purification Program is labor-intensive and you're buying both the product and the protocol. Other SKUs are harder to justify versus retail alternatives. The clinician's review walks through this product by product.

How does it compare to brands I can find online?

Versus brands like Garden of Life or MegaFood that you can buy at Whole Foods or Amazon — Standard Process's farm-to-tablet sourcing is more integrated and the excipient list is cleaner (no magnesium stearate, no titanium dioxide), but you pay more and need a practitioner. Versus Thorne and Pure Encapsulations — those are also practitioner-channel but they go with isolated nutrients (like methylated B12) rather than whole-food concentrates. Versus NOW or Nature Made — different philosophy entirely; those are high-dose synthetics at retail price.
