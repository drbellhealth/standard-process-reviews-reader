Standard Process Ingredients

A line-by-line look at what's inside Standard Process whole-food supplements (brand-wide review hub), including active components and excipients.

The whole-food philosophy is the main thing to know. Standard Process gets nutrients from concentrated whole foods, not from synthetic single-molecule isolates. That's what makes the brand different from NOW or Nature Made. Whether that matters for you depends on what you're trying to do.

Active Ingredients

The kinds of ingredients you'll see across the catalog:

  • Whole-food vegetable concentrates — beet, carrot, alfalfa, peas, oats, brussels sprouts, kale; many grown on their own organic farm
  • Whole-food animal concentrates — bovine adrenal, liver, kidney; New Zealand sourced
  • PMG extracts — Thytrophin PMG, Drenatrophin PMG, Cardiotrophin PMG, Ovatrophin PMG; their proprietary nuclear-tissue category
  • Glandulars — Drenamin (adrenal), Symplex F (female), Symplex M (male); dried whole-organ tablets
  • MediHerb herbals — Andrographis Complex, Boswellia Complex, Echinacea Premium, Bacopa Complex, ResCo, Phytolyn
  • Targeted formulas — Allerplex (respiratory), Antronex (natural antihistamine), Arginex (kidneys), Cholacol (gallbladder)
  • Multi-vitamins — Catalyn from 1929, Cyrofood, Bio-Dent, Trace Minerals-B12
  • 21-Day Purification Program — SP Cleanse, SP Complete shake, Whole Food Fiber, Gastro-Fiber
  • Pediatric — Catalyn Chewable, Tuna Omega-3 Chewable, Calcifood

Other Ingredients (Excipients)

Standard Process's inactive ingredient lists are cleaner than most. Most tablets use honey or vegetable-source calcium stearate as the binder instead of magnesium stearate. No synthetic colorants. No titanium dioxide. No shellac. No gluten in most SKUs. Capsules are gelatin or vegetable cellulose. They publish the full per-product allergen table in their practitioner reference — most supplement brands don't.

Allergens and Sensitivities

Most multi-vitamin and broad-spectrum stuff is gluten-free, soy-free, and dairy-free. A small number of legacy SKUs have soy or whey — check the table. Glandulars and PMG products have bovine tissue and aren't appropriate for vegetarians, vegans, or anyone with a beef allergy. MediHerb plant formulas — standard herb-class allergies apply, especially ragweed family. Pediatric chewables sweeten with honey or fruit juice rather than synthetic sweeteners or sugar alcohols. The brand doesn't carry NSF Certified for Sport on most SKUs — if you're a competitive athlete subject to drug testing, verify with your team.

Sourcing and Quality Notes

The 420-acre Palmyra Wisconsin organic farm is real. You can tour it. They grow beets, carrots, alfalfa, peas, oats, brussels sprouts, kale, buckwheat. Harvest-to-processing happens in hours, not days. The bovine glandular and PMG raw material comes from New Zealand BSE-free certified herds. The MediHerb sub-line carries chromatography paperwork meeting pharmaceutical botanical standards. How Ingredients Compare to Similar Products

How Standard Process compares to brands you've probably heard of: Thorne and Pure Encapsulations — also practitioner-channel, typically cheaper, but they lead with isolated nutrients (methyl-B12, K2 MK-7) rather than whole-food concentrates. Garden of Life and MegaFood — retail-channel whole-food brands, less vertically integrated sourcing, easier to buy. NOW and Nature Made — high-dose isolated synthetics at retail price, totally different philosophy. Apex Energetics and Biotics Research — closest philosophical peers to Standard Process in the practitioner-channel whole-food space. The 21-Day Purification Program lines up against Thorne Mediclear and Designs for Health PaleoCleanse; Standard Process's version is the most structured and most expensive.
